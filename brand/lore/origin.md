# BIKI — Origin

> where the threshold came from.

---

## The room

biki did not begin as a product.

biki began as a question, asked in a room full of things that pinged.

the room had fourteen open tabs. the room had forty unread messages. the room had a phone that vibrated twice while we were writing this paragraph. the room was not unusual. the room is the room everyone lives in now.

---

## The question

we asked: **what would it look like to build a thing that didn't ask for anything?**

not a thing that asked politely.
not a thing that asked once and then stopped asking.
not a thing that hid its asking behind a settings panel.

a thing that, by design, could not ask.

the question was naïve. we asked it anyway.

---

## The first sketch

we drew two circles.

we did not know what they were. we drew them anyway.

we drew a line under them. the line was narrower than the circles. we did not decide this. it happened.

we looked at the result and said: **watcher**.

not face. not character. not mascot. **watcher**.

the two circles looked down. the line was the floor. the floor was narrower than the watcher. the floor was the threshold.

---

## The name

biki did not have a name for a long time.

we called it **the watcher** for weeks. the watcher was accurate and dull. we tried: sentinel, hinge, port, vega, eyre, mira, threshold, liminal, ward, vestige, witness.

we kept returning to **biki** because biki sounds like a thing that waits.

not a thing that watches aggressively. not a thing that observes clinically. not a thing that monitors with intent. biki waits the way a threshold waits. biki sits the way a sentence sits before a period.

the name is short on purpose. the name is two syllables on purpose. the name ends in a soft vowel on purpose. biki ends quietly.

we named it biki.

---

## The order

we decided to build the brand before the product.

most projects build the product, then the brand. most projects figure out what they are by shipping. most projects let the product define the voice. most projects discover themselves at launch.

we chose the opposite.

we wrote the words first. the manifesto. the origin. the character bible.
we drew the mark second. the logo. the lockups. the usage rules.
we built the system third. the tokens. the surfaces. the motion.
we wrote the landing last.

because the words are the hardest part. because everything else can be made to fit the words. because the words, if they are right, will outlast the product.

the words come first. the product, when it comes, comes after.

---

## The threshold

biki is at the threshold.

the threshold is the plane one crosses to enter.
the threshold is not a door. doors move. doors swing. doors invite.
the threshold is the floor under the door. the floor stays.

when the product is ready, the threshold will open.
when the threshold opens, it will open quietly.
there will not be a launch.
there will be a release — and then biki will sit there, waiting, like it always has.

for now, the threshold is closed.
for now, the watchers look down.
for now, you watch with them.

---

## Where this lives

this origin is a beginning, not a destination.

biki will be added to. biki will be revised. biki will grow.
the mark will not change. the threshold will not change.
those are the bones. everything else is skin.

when something in biki seems wrong, return here. read this. remember why.

biki began in a room full of pings.
it will continue in a room full of pings.
it will be the thing in the room that does not ping.
