# BIKI — Manifesto

> presence, not interruption.

---

## One

most things demand attention.

most things shout.

most things want to be the loudest thing on your screen.

biki does not.

---

## Two

attention is the only currency that cannot be refunded.

once spent, it is gone. the things that spend it for you — pings, badges, badges-with-counts, alerts, red dots, vibration — spend it without asking. they treat your attention as a resource to extract.

biki does not extract attention.
biki waits for you to bring it.

---

## Three

the threshold is the plane one crosses to enter.

before any action there is a moment.
before any message there is a breath.
before any decision there is a stillness.

biki sits at that plane.
biki watches that plane.
biki does not cross it for you.

---

## Four

we believe restraint is the design.

we believe patience is a feature.
we believe presence is worth more than interruption.
we believe the mark that waits is worth more than the notification that pushes.
we believe a thing that asks for nothing is rarer than a thing that asks for everything.

the watcher is rare because the watcher is hard to build. most products are built to interrupt. they are optimized for attention extraction. they are measured by the number of times they pulled you back in.

biki is built to watch.
biki is optimized for restraint.
biki is measured by the number of times it did not.

---

## Five

this is not a manifesto for a product.

this is a manifesto for a presence.

biki will not be the loudest thing in your stack. biki will not be the fastest. biki will not be the most addictive. biki will not be the thing that makes you check your screen more often.

biki will be the thing that waited.
biki will be the thing that watched you cross the threshold.
biki will be the thing that knew — and let you decide.

---

## Six

the mark is two watchers above a threshold.

the watchers look down.
the threshold holds.
nothing crosses without you.

this is biki.
